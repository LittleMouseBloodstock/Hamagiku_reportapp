# AGENTS.md

## Shinba Development Context

Before making product, UI, copy, or architecture decisions in this repository, treat Shinba as a trust-focused equine operations and bloodstock intelligence platform.

Shinba is not a generic AI app, generic translation app, generic chat app, or generic task manager. It exists to turn fragmented racehorse field information into trusted reports, multilingual handoffs, and decision-ready data.

The detailed vision document is `docs/SHINBA_VISION.md`. The brand architecture document is `docs/brand-architecture.md`.

## Product Priorities

1. Field usability comes first.
2. Trust and responsibility come before automation.
3. Preserve equine, veterinary, owner-reporting, and handoff context.
4. Build small usable slices before broad management systems.
5. Structure information so it can become a future data asset.

## Current Product Pillars

- Shinba Report: owner reporting, monthly updates, international owner communication.
- Shinba Handoff: multilingual instruction sharing, confirmation, completion reporting, and manager confirmation.
- Bloodstock Intelligence: future decision support for sales, breeding, investment, and risk review.

## Copy And UX Rules

- Do not lead with "AI" as the primary value proposition.
- Prefer "reduce misunderstandings," "improve reporting quality," "preserve judgment material," and "support expert decisions."
- Do not imply Shinba replaces veterinarians, farm staff, managers, trainers, owners, or bloodstock experts.
- Keep original records, edits, confirmations, and responsible humans visible where trust matters.
- Separate "seen," "understood," "done," "completion reported," and "confirmed by manager" in Handoff-related design.
- Avoid heavy desktop-first workflows for field staff. Prefer smartphone-first, quick input, voice, photos, templates, and one-tap actions.

## Brand And Routing Rules

- `Shinba` is the parent brand, not one product.
- `shinba.app` is the parent brand entry point.
- Product workflows should move toward product-specific destinations or subdomains when they become app-like.
- Avoid wording that makes `shinba.app` sound like only `Shinba Report`.
