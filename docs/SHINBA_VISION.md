# Shinba Business Vision / Mission / Purpose

## Purpose

Shinba exists to reduce information gaps, language gaps, and judgment gaps in racehorse operations.

Racehorse work involves veterinarians, farm staff, managers, owners, trainers, overseas partners, and bloodstock professionals. Important information is often scattered across conversations, LINE messages, photos, paper notes, spreadsheets, and individual memory. Language differences and role differences can prevent important details from being understood at the right time.

Shinba is not a generic translation app, reporting tool, chat app, or task manager. It is a group of products that turns fragmented on-farm information into trusted reports, handoffs, and decision-ready data.

The long-term purpose is to build a reliable intelligence layer for racehorse operations and bloodstock decisions by structuring field information from both practical and expert perspectives.

## Vision

Shinba aims to create a world where important information about racehorses is communicated accurately, preserved, and used for better decisions across languages, roles, and borders.

Japan's racehorse industry has strong field skills, careful horse management, and deep practical knowledge. Much of that value is still not fully digitized, multilingual, or structured as reusable data. Shinba should not erase field knowledge. It should make that knowledge visible, transferable, and useful for the next generation.

Shinba's long-term vision is to become a trusted intelligence foundation connecting field information and decision-making in the racehorse business.

## Mission

Shinba's mission is to build products that convert information generated in racehorse operations into formats that can be used for reporting, sharing, handoff, and judgment.

The current product themes are:

### Owner Reporting

Turn field notes, photos, body weight, veterinary observations, and monthly updates into natural Japanese, English, or bilingual reports for owners and overseas stakeholders.

This is not simple translation. The goal is to preserve field nuance while restructuring information into a form the recipient can understand and trust.

### Multilingual Handoff

Support instructions, cautions, confirmations, and completion reports between farms, stables, veterinarians, and foreign staff across different native languages.

The goal is not generic task management. The goal is to reduce missed instructions, missed confirmations, and unclear responsibility.

The following states must be treated separately:

- Seen
- Understood
- Done
- Completion reported
- Confirmed by a manager

### Bloodstock Intelligence

In the future, Shinba should structure treatment history, body information, pedigree, development records, reporting history, and risk factors to support sales, breeding, investment, and bloodstock decisions.

Shinba should not automatically judge a horse's value. The goal is Data Input + Expert Interpretation: a system that helps specialists make better decisions.

## Product Philosophy

### Do not increase field burden

Racehorse operations are busy. A product that requires too much typing, complex workflows, desktop-only usage, or administrator-first thinking will fail.

Prioritize smartphone use, short interactions, voice input, photos, templates, and one-tap actions.

### Convert business context, not just language

Shinba is not a simple Japanese-English translation app. It must understand equine field context, veterinary context, owner-reporting context, and handoff context.

Field notes and owner-facing reports are different forms of communication. Shinba should absorb that difference.

### Do not over-emphasize AI

AI is a means, not the value proposition. Users care that reports are faster, communication mistakes decrease, English support becomes easier, and judgment materials are preserved.

Public copy should emphasize trust, clarity, field fit, and operational quality rather than "AI automation."

### Do not replace expert judgment

Shinba does not replace veterinarians, farm staff, managers, owners, trainers, or bloodstock experts.

It organizes information, reduces missed details, and makes the next action easier.

### Trust comes first

Racehorse business is a trust business. Poor translation, inaccurate reports, data leakage, excessive AI confidence, and unclear responsibility can create serious problems.

Shinba should prioritize:

- Data safety
- Access control
- Original text preservation
- Edit history
- Records of who confirmed what
- UI that avoids over-trusting generated output
- Final confirmation by the responsible human

## Target Users

Primary users include:

- Training farms
- Breeding farms
- Racing stables
- Veterinarians
- Farm managers
- Foreign staff
- Overseas owners
- Domestic owners
- Bloodstock agents
- Future users involved in sales, breeding, and investment decisions

Initial priority should stay narrow:

1. Farms and stakeholders burdened by reporting to overseas owners
2. Farms and stables struggling with multilingual instruction sharing and handoff

## What Shinba Is Not

Shinba is not:

- A generic translation app
- A generic chat app
- A generic task manager
- A generic farm management system
- A black-box AI that automatically judges horse value
- A heavy management tool that forces large amounts of field input
- A horse-racing betting or prediction service
- A medical AI replacing veterinary diagnosis

Shinba converts racehorse field information into trusted operational information that stakeholders can use.

## Decision Principles For Development

### 1. Field usability first

If a feature is too complex for real field use, it is not good enough. Prioritize smartphone usability, fast input, and clear actions.

### 2. Do not damage trust

Avoid designs that create misunderstanding in generated reports, translation, status changes, or permission handling. Preserve originals, histories, and confirmations.

### 3. Preserve equine business context

Do not copy generic SaaS or task-management patterns without adapting them. Horses, people, deadlines, treatments, cautions, photos, confirmations, and completion reports matter.

### 4. Build small first

Do not start with a giant operations platform. Validate the strongest workflow problems in small, usable slices.

### 5. Build toward future data assets

Short-term convenience should also help structure reporting history, treatment history, handoff history, and horse-level state changes for future decision support.
